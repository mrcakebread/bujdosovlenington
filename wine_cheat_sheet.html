For Beginners

Your Tasting "Safe Words"

Use these descriptors to talk about wine with confidence. They are benchmarks to help you describe what you're sensing. Wine doesn't actually contain these fruits; it just reminds us of them.

The "Dry" Myth: Most wine is dry. If you think you dislike "dry," you likely just dislike bitterness. Trust your own nose—if you and your friend smell different things, that's okay. These guides are here to make you comfortable, not to tell you what to think.

Chardonnay ▼
Fruit: Apple, Lemon, Pineapple
Floral: Honeysuckle
Non-Fruit: Butter
Profile: Oak / Full / Fruity
Sauvignon Blanc ▼
Fruit: Grapefruit, Lime, Passionfruit
Floral: Elderflower
Non-Fruit: Grass
Profile: No Oak / Light / Mineral
Riesling ▼
Fruit: Apricot, Nectarine, Lime
Floral: Jasmine
Non-Fruit: Petrol
Profile: No Oak / Light / Fruity
Pinot Grigio ▼
Fruit: Lemon, Pear, White Peach
Floral: Honeysuckle
Non-Fruit: Saline
Profile: No Oak / Light / Mineral
Pinot Noir ▼
Fruit: Cherry, Raspberry, Cranberry
Floral: Violet
Non-Fruit: Forest Floor
Profile: Oak / Light / Mineral
Cabernet ▼
Fruit: Blackcurrant, Blackberry, Black Cherry
Floral: Violet
Non-Fruit: Cedar
Profile: Oak / Full / Fruity
Malbec ▼
Fruit: Plum, Blackberry, Blueberry
Floral: Violet
Non-Fruit: Cocoa
Profile: Oak / Full / Fruity
Sangiovese ▼
